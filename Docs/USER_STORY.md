- Jordan loses their place on the page and has to re-read sentences

- Jordan is embarrassed when classmates notice and ask questions

- Dad wants to help but doesn't know what to do when Jordan gets frustrated

### Emotionally
- Jordan knows the blinking isn't "on purpose" but still feels embarrassed
- Some days Jordan avoids reading out loud in class to prevent the tic from getting worse
- Jordan wonders if the tics will ever go away or get worse
- Feels alone â€” doesn't know anyone else with Tourette's

## Design Implications

- **Language:** Grade 5 reading level. No medical jargon without explanation.
- **Tone:** Warm, encouraging, never clinical. Like a supportive older friend.
- **Visuals:** Bright but not overstimulating. Soft gradients, friendly icons.
- **Interactions:** Simple, low-friction. A 10-year-old should be able to log a tic in under 10 seconds.
- **Privacy:** Jordan's tic data stays private. No sharing without consent.
- **Dual audience:** The app serves both Jordan AND Jordan's parents â€” different sections, same compassionate tone.
